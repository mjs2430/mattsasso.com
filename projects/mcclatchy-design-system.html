One of my long-term projects with McClatchy is our subscription platform. This complicated product has many different moving parts that all mesh together to connect our subscriptions across all devices and sites. All 30 McClatchy markets use this, and we offer different subscription packages, offers, and ways to subscribe. This project takes a lot of UX research, user studies, and planning to achieve our goals. I've worked with our DSP lead devs to create a style guide specific to our subscription platform, and am now circling back with another lead dev to integrate our DSP style guide within our Saratoga Design System

I also helped setup a space for our Email Design System (EDS) that a colleague of mine worked on. I taught her how to use Hugo, Git, GitHub and work with ShadowDOM and HTML Custom Elements so she could create her wonderful style guide.

We use Hugo to create the style guide for our design system, and host everything on GitHub. I currently have my own forked repo for all of the major changes we are implementing as I merge our DSP style guide into SDS. This is just step one, as we will be onboarding devs later on and tweaking the system to further integrate tokens and better variables.

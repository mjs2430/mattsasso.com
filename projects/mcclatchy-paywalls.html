mcclatchy paywalls

2019 | ui, ux, prototyping view prototype

One of my long-term projects with McClatchy is our subscription platform. This complicated product has many different moving parts that all mesh together to connect our subscriptions across all devices and sites. All 30 McClatchy markets use this, and we offer different subscription packages, offers, and ways to subscribe. This project takes a lot of UX research, user studies, and planning to achieve our goals. We used guidelines from our Saratoga design system to get started.

My responsibilities include involvement in everything from the previously mentioned UX/planning stages, to being responsible for wireframes, hi-fidelity mocks, prototyping (functional mocks and functional code examples) and development handoff. I've worked on paywalls, my account pages, sign in flows, purchase flows, subscription landing pages, AMP paywalls, Facebook paywalls, and every step of the user journey that revolves around these products.

Our new paywall system is the first step in the complete overhaul of our subscription platform. We have multiple entitlement levels for users, and our system needs to accomadate all scenarios. You can see multiple iterations of mocks in Sketch here.

I am currently working on iterating on this paywall and we will soon launch V2 that will improve eye-tracking on desktop, create easier A/B testing and optimization tests for colors, copy and images, as well as help the paywalls simplicity on mobile. To be developed soon.

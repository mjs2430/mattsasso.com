Overview

My work on the TELUS Agent Trainer LLM project successfully transitioned from an 8 week proof of concept into a permanent, enterprise-grade product used for training call center agents globally. As the Lead AI Product Consultant, I drove the strategic architecture and was recognized as an authority in prompt engineering, guiding software teams through the shift to AI first development. This project's success demonstrated massive organizational scaling, moving beyond an innovative experiment to become a critical solution for agent evaluation and operational efficiency.

Key Accomplishments

GenAI Innovation & Enterprise Scaling

Led the initial 8-week LLM Based Agent Training POC for a Fortune 500 telecommunications client. The architecture and strategy were so highly validated that the prototype successfully scaled into a permanent, enterprise-wide product used to train and evaluate call center agents globally.

Technical Leadership & Prompt Engineering

Recognized by the internal GenAI center of excellence as a foremost authority in prompt engineering. Guided software engineering teams through the paradigm shift from traditional coding to AI-first development, establishing best practices for structured prompting (e.g., using JSON/XML tags) and constraint-based instructions to drastically reduce AI hallucinations.

AI Architecture & Middleware

Strategized the technical infrastructure by conceptualizing a middleware layer to process user inputs, interface with LLM APIs, and execute logic based on the model's responses. This allowed the system to seamlessly map indeterminate natural language into deterministic Decision Tree paths.

Quality Assurance & AI Evaluation Frameworks

Pioneered a net-new QA methodology for generative outputs. Designed a rigorous automated evaluation framework utilizing "Gold Standard" conversational inputs and responses, allowing the system to objectively grade the simulated customer's performance, mood progression, and issue resolution accuracy against established baselines.
